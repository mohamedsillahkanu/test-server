🌍 Detailed Epidemiological Assessment

📊 Current Burden Overview

  • Annual Parasite Incidence: 120 cases per 1,000 population
  • Case Fatality Rate: 0.65% (target: <0.5%)
  • Test Positivity Rate: 23% (indicating active transmission)
  • Confirmed Cases (2024): 15,234 (↓8% from 2023)

⚠️ High-Risk Populations

  • Children <5 years: 45% of all cases, 60% of deaths
  • Pregnant Women: 8% higher risk of severe malaria
  • Rural Populations: 78% of cases in rural areas
  • Mobile Populations: 15% higher transmission risk

🌍 Geographic Patterns

  • Northern Region: Highest burden (180‰ incidence)
  • Eastern Region: Moderate-high (140‰ incidence)
  • Border Areas: Cross-border transmission hotspots
  • Urban Areas: Emerging peri-urban transmission

🌦️ Environmental Factors

  • Rainfall: Peak transmission follows rainy season (Jun-Sep)
  • Temperature: Optimal range 20-30°C increasing vector activity
  • Altitude: <1,500m elevation zones most affected
  • Water Bodies: Proximity to dams/irrigation increases risk 3x
