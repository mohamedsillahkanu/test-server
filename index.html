📊 Situation Analysis
Epidemiological Overview:

The country continues to experience moderate to high malaria transmission with significant regional variations. Current analysis shows:
  • Incidence rate: 120 cases per 1,000 population at risk
  • Mortality rate: 0.8 deaths per 1,000 population at risk
  • Peak transmission season: June to November
  • Most affected age group: Children under 5 years (45% of cases)
  • Geographic hotspots: Northern and eastern regions
Key Risk Factors Contributing to Transmission:

Environmental Factors:
  • Seasonal rainfall patterns creating breeding sites
  • Temperature variations affecting vector survival
  • Altitude and ecological zones
  • Proximity to water bodies and irrigation systems

Socio-economic Factors:
  • Rural populations with limited healthcare access
  • Poor housing conditions
  • Limited education and awareness
  • Economic barriers to prevention and treatment

🔍 Surveillance Systems
Surveillance Infrastructure:

The national malaria surveillance system operates through multiple channels:
  • Health Facility Reporting: 850+ facilities reporting monthly
  • Community Case Management: 2,400+ community health workers
  • Sentinel Surveillance: 25 sentinel sites for enhanced monitoring
  • Active Case Detection: Reactive and proactive case finding
  • Cross-border Surveillance: 12 border entry points monitored
Current Data Quality Metrics:

Areas for Improvement:

🏥 Case Management
Current Treatment Guidelines (WHO 2023):

Uncomplicated P. falciparum:
  • First-line: Artemether-lumefantrine (AL) 6-dose regimen
  • Alternative: Artesunate-amodiaquine (ASAQ)
  • Weight-based dosing for children

Severe Malaria:
  • IV Artesunate 2.4mg/kg at 0, 12, and 24 hours
  • Then oral ACT once able to tolerate
  • Supportive care and complications management

Special Populations:
  • Pregnant women: Artesunate-based treatment
  • Children <5kg: Specialized pediatric formulations
Diagnostic Strategy:

Parasitological Confirmation Required:
  • Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs): P.f/P.v combination tests
  • Microscopy: Gold standard where available
  • PCR: For surveillance and research purposes

Quality Assurance:
• External quality assurance program for microscopy
• RDT lot testing and storage monitoring
• Regular supportive supervision visits
• Proficiency testing for laboratory technicians

🦟 Vector Control
LLIN Program Status:

Coverage Metrics:
  • Household ownership: 67% (target: >80%)
  • Population access: 52% (target: >80%)
  • Usage rate: 78% of owned nets
  • Good physical condition: 84% of nets

Distribution Strategy:
• Mass distribution campaigns every 3 years
• Continuous distribution through ANC and EPI
• School-based distribution for hard-to-reach areas
• Community-based distribution in remote regions
IRS Implementation:

Current Operations:
  • Target districts: 15 high-burden districts
  • Structures sprayed: 245,000+ (85% coverage)
  • Population protected: 1.2 million people
  • Insecticide used: Pirimiphos-methyl (Actellic 300CS)

Quality Control:
• Pre-spray community mobilization
• Daily supervision and quality checks
• Post-spray coverage validation
• Residual efficacy monitoring
Resistance Status:

Key Findings:
  • Pyrethroid resistance: Confirmed in 12/15 sites
  • DDT resistance: Widespread (mortality <80%)
  • Carbamate susceptibility: Maintained in most areas
  • Organophosphate susceptibility: Variable results

Response Strategy:
• Rotation of insecticide classes for IRS
• Introduction of next-generation LLINs
• Enhanced vector surveillance
• Resistance management guidelines implementation
Environmental Management:

• Identification and mapping of breeding sites
• Community-based larval control programs
• Environmental modification projects
• Inter-sectoral collaboration with water and agriculture ministries

Pilot Programs:
• Urban malaria control in 3 cities
• Larvicide application in irrigation schemes
• Community education on water storage practices

📋 Strategic Planning
Strategic Plan 2021-2025:

Vision: A malaria-free nation by 2030

Strategic Objectives:
  1. Reduce malaria morbidity and mortality by 60%
  2. Strengthen health systems for malaria control
  3. Improve vector control coverage and effectiveness
  4. Enhance surveillance and response systems
  5. Foster research and innovation
  6. Ensure sustainable financing

Implementation Phases:
• Phase 1 (2021-2023): Scale-up and strengthening
• Phase 2 (2024-2025): Consolidation and optimization
• Phase 3 (2026-2030): Pre-elimination activities
2024 Operational Plan Highlights:

Key Activities:
  • Universal LLIN campaign in 8 regions (Q2-Q3)
  • IRS implementation in 15 high-burden districts
  • Seasonal malaria chemoprevention in Sahel regions
  • Health worker capacity building program
  • Community case management expansion

Budget Allocation:
• Vector control: 45% ($12.5M)
• Case management: 25% ($6.8M)
• Surveillance & M&E: 15% ($4.1M)
• Health system strengthening: 10% ($2.7M)
• Program management: 5% ($1.4M)
Funding Sources (2024):

Total Budget: $27.5 Million

  • Global Fund: $18.2M (66%)
  • Government budget: $4.8M (17%)
  • USAID/PMI: $2.9M (11%)
  • World Bank: $1.2M (4%)
  • Other partners: $0.4M (2%)

Sustainability Plan:
• Gradual increase in domestic financing
• Integration with universal health coverage
• Private sector engagement initiatives
• Resource mobilization from new partners
Risk Assessment & Mitigation:

High-Priority Risks:
  • Funding gaps: Diversify funding sources, advocacy
  • Insecticide resistance: Resistance management strategy
  • Climate change: Adaptive programming, early warning
  • Health system capacity: Workforce development, infrastructure
  • Political instability: Contingency planning, local partnerships

Contingency Plans:
• Emergency response protocols
• Alternative implementation strategies
• Risk monitoring and early warning systems
